Le Petit Croissant

UX & Ordering Flow Evaluation

An aesthetic and functional critique of the user journey,
with targeted recommendations for conversion improvement.

Executive Summary

Le Petit Croissant’s website effectively communicates its high-end, artisanal brand through excellent imagery. The overall structure is clean and professional.

However, the ordering flow introduces significant friction early in the journey. Users are forced through a mandatory "Gatekeeper Modal" (Delivery vs. Pickup selection), only to be met with disorienting page reloads, unclickable products, and hidden stock limitations later on.

Core Objective

Fix the fragmented user journey by deferring logistical selection, restoring clickable product modals, and enforcing transparent location-based inventory upfront.

1. Homepage & Initial Discovery

Discovery Phase
  • Brand Alignment: High-quality, appetizing imagery instantly establishes trust and product quality.
  • Clear Value Prop: "Early-morning delivery for corporate meetings" is prominently communicated.
  • Weak primary CTA: The "Click to see our full menu" link is small text. It struggles to compete visually with the surrounding heavy imagery.

Quick Win

Upgrade the text link to a high-contrast, primary button (e.g., solid orange/brand color) to draw the eye immediately.

2. The "Gatekeeper" Modal

High Friction Point

When a user attempts to order, they immediately hit a barrier. The flow requires excessive steps just to set a preference:

  1. Click "Order Now"
  2. Select "Pickup" or "Delivery"
  3. Wait for map/locations to update
  4. Click the radio button for the specific location
  5. Click "Next", then Click "Save"
  6. Disorientation: The page completely reloads from the top instead of smoothly scrolling.

Impact: 6 clicks required just to start. The subsequent full-page reload breaks momentum and disorients the user entirely.

3. Menu & Product Discovery

Broken Interaction
  • Sticky Navigation: Category navigation anchors at the top, allowing for rapid jumping.
  • False "Dead Ends": Modals contain mandatory checkboxes (e.g. 48h pre-order). If missed, clicking "Add to Cart" does nothing.
  • Impact: Users assume the site is broken and abandon discovery.

4. Cart & Checkout State

Critical Flaw
  • Cart Drawer: Efficient side-drawer design prevents losing place.
  • Volatile Cart State: Changing locations or delivery methods mid-session clears all items from the cart instantly.
  • Impact: Destroys built purchase momentum.

Animated log showing the cart wipe

Strategic Action Plan

1. Fix the Journey Mechanics (Critical)

Highlight mandatory form fields inside the product modals to stop the "Add to Cart" button from feeling broken. Stop full-page reloads after the location form—smoothly auto-scroll the user back down to the menu grid instead.

2. Ensure Cart Persistence

Never wipe a user's cart simply because they interacted with the location/delivery method header. If inventory relies on location, flag incompatible items *inside* the cart view rather than silently deleting everything.

3. Defer Logistical Framing

Whenever possible, allow users to add items to their cart friction-free. Only trigger the heavy Delivery/Pickup workflow when they attempt to click "Checkout".

Thank You

Implementing these focused UX improvements will drastically reduce
early drop-offs and improve the overall conversion rate.
